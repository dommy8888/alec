Following the ceremony, the celebration continues with a garden party at a picturesque vineyard. The evening will feature wine tastings, traditional Italian dances, and a luxurious outdoor dining experience with gourmet cuisine.

Sardinian Wedding Traditions

Traditional Dress and Jewelry

The bride and groom often wear traditional Sardinian attire, featuring intricate embroidery and gold filigree jewelry, signifying wealth and beauty. The bride's jewelry, often passed down through generations, is an important part of Sardinian culture.

Breaking of the Plate (Sa Ratzia)

In Sardinia, the "Sa Ratzia" custom involves breaking a plate filled with rice, wheat, or petals at the feet of the couple. This symbolizes divine grace and is repeated until the plate is completely shattered. The breaking of the plate signifies good fortune and a blessed future for the couple.

Chaining the Couple

Some Sardinian weddings feature a symbolic act of chaining the couple together, representing their eternal bond and shared destiny. The chains are often symbolic and are removed at the end of the ceremony, marking the beginning of their new life together.

Fertility and Prosperity Symbols

At a Sardinian wedding, rice and wheat are frequently used during the ceremony as symbols of fertility and prosperity. These items are often sprinkled over the couple, ensuring a future of abundance and the blessing of a fruitful life together.

The Serenade

In a romantic Sardinian tradition, the groom often surprises the bride with a serenade the night before the wedding. This serenade, typically performed with musicians, adds a personal and heartfelt touch to the pre-wedding celebration, strengthening the bond between the couple.
